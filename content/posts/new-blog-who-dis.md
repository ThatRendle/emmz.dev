## Projects I'm working on

### New workshop

So the theme for this blog was actually extracted from a "design language"
that Claude and the Anthropic `frontend-design` skill came up with for the
website for the Claude Code workshop that I've been working on with my
longtime friend and collaborator Dylan Beattie. Check it out at
[claude.rendle.dev](https://claude.rendle.dev)

We're going to be running public workshops online that anyone can join,
usually using a two-half-days format, sometimes on weekends. And for
businesses, we can run a single day either online or at a location of
your choosing, for up to 20 attendees, with the content customised for
your teams and your projects.

I personally believe a live workshop is a better fit for teaching this
material, purely because it's all moving and changing so fast that a
pre-recorded video course might be out of date a week after we finish
editing it. This way, everything stays right up-to-date, even if we
have to update the demos or the slides the night before the workshop.

We've run an in-person one for a customer in Sweden and it went really
well. The attendees were a mix of developers and engineering-adjacent
people, and by the end of the day everyone completed their final
projects - I won't spoil what that is, but it was a lot of fun.

### Dmon

I've been working on my own agent harness and routing system, built
with .NET 10 and Microsoft.Extensions.AI. There's an ulterior motive
that I'm not ready to share just yet, but I can tell you it's heavily
focused on privacy and local models like Gemma 4 that you can run on
a Mac Mini with 48GB of unified memory. The stuff I'm learning while
working on that is the reason I wanted to start this blog.

I think that the end-game of all this is more specialised small models
running on commodity hardware, like Apple Silicon or Nvidia's new
Arm-based CPU/GPU/thing. Maybe not this year or next, but the
performance and the memory available, once the chip shortages
are resolved, are going to keep going up, and the models are going
to keep getting more efficient. The Mac Studio with the M3 Ultra
chip and 512GB of unified memory was "only" £10k; rumour has it
the M5 Ultra version will go to 768GB. At some point that capacity,
combined with falling prices, will make it possible for everyone
to run frontier-level models on a relatively low-power system that
sits in their house like a HomePod or an Echo does today.

You can give these local models access to your emails, your calendar,
your messages, all your documents and other data without it ever
leaving your house. Nothing uploaded to The Cloud, no worries about
what Zuckerberg or Altman or whoever might do with it. Your model,
your data, your hardware. That's what I'm trying to build.
Obviously I'm hoping to make a bit of money off it somehow, but
the core of it is [open source](https://github.com/daemonicai/dmon-core)
and will stay that way.

### Incredibly personalised apps

I got back on stage to do stand-up comedy again recently, after a
25 year absence from that scene. Back in the day, we used to write
new material on our arms or on scraps of paper. This time I made
an iPhone app I called BitTimer, where you could paste a Markdown
bullet list of "bits" and it would step through them one by one,
with a timer on the top half of the screen. It's a native iPhone app,
SwiftUI, Xcode, all that stuff. It took maybe half an hour of my time,
and two or three hours of elapsed time.

Now I'm building ADHD Buddy, for iPhone and Apple Watch, to remind me
to do things like take medications or do breathing exercises or drink
water or whatever. It's a bit more complicated but it's still just
scratching an itch. I might publish it. We'll see.
